## Reading

In an age of instant messages and endless recommendations, quiet work has become less visible, but not less valuable. A person who studies a theorem, rewrites a paragraph, or learns a new language often makes progress that cannot be photographed at once. The change is gradual: a sharper question, a better sentence, a calmer response to difficulty.

Noise is not only sound. It is also the pressure to display every step before the step has become meaningful. Quiet work gives ideas enough time to become solid. It protects attention from being spent too cheaply, and it reminds us that depth usually arrives after repetition, not before it.

The challenge, then, is not to disappear from the world, but to choose when to be reachable and when to be absorbed. A thoughtful life needs both windows and walls: windows to meet others, walls to keep the mind from being scattered by every passing signal.

## Word Bank

| Word | Meaning | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| visible | easy to notice or see | Quiet progress is not always visible. |
| gradual | happening slowly over time | Language learning is a gradual process. |
| display | to show something publicly | We do not need to display every draft. |
| solid | strong, reliable, or well developed | Repetition makes an idea solid. |
| absorbed | deeply interested and focused | She was absorbed in the problem. |
| scattered | spread in many directions; not focused | Too many alerts leave attention scattered. |

## Sentence Lab

> A person who studies a theorem, rewrites a paragraph, or learns a new language often makes progress that cannot be photographed at once.

这个句子用三个并列动作铺开“quiet work”的范围：studies, rewrites, learns。后面的定语从句 `that cannot be photographed at once` 强调真正的进步不一定立刻可见。

> A thoughtful life needs both windows and walls.

这里用隐喻表达平衡：windows 代表连接，walls 代表边界。写作时这种短句适合作为段落的收束句。

## Reflection

今天可以想一个问题：我最近有哪些进步是“不能立刻拍照”的？把它写下来，也许它正在变成更稳定的东西。
